$$S(n,k) = \frac{1}{k!}\sum_{j=0}^k (-1)^{k-j}\binom{k}{j}j^n$$

That's the beautiful equation I was talking about. Here $S(\cdot,\cdot)$ is the Stirling number of the second kind.
Its proof is based around counting surjective functions $f:[n]\to[k]$.
